Casting Spells of a Higher Level

  • Spellcasters, if they've discovered a spell that's too powerful for them, can combine spell slots to cast the spell together.
    Each spellcaster contributing a spell slot must pass an Arcana check for the spell to be successfully cast.
    Alternatively, a single spellcaster may combine multiple slots of various levels to cast higher level spells in the same way.
    • Whether the spell fails or succeeds, the contributed spell slots are expended
    • Arcana Check w/ Multiple Casters: DC 5 + Spell Level + Contributed Spell Slot Level
      • E.G. A group of 3 casters is trying to cast a 10th-level spell.
        George will contribute a 5th-level spell slot, Kara will contribute a 3rd-level spell slot, and Selina will contribute a 2nd-level spell slot.
        • George: DC 5 + 10 + 5 = DC 20 Arcana Check
        • Kara: DC 5 + 10 + 3 = DC 18 Arcana Check
        • Selina: DC 5 + 10 + 2 = DC 17 Arcana Check
    • Arcana Check as a Solo Caster: DC 5 + Spell Level + Sum of Contributed Spell Slot Levels
      • E.G. Casper wants to cast an 8th-level spell, but only has access to 5th-level spell slots.
        So, Casper will contribute one 5th-level spell slot and one 3rd-level spell slot.
        • Casper: DC 5 + 8 + 5 + 3 = DC 21 Arcana Check
    • Determining Success: All checks must succeed for the spell to be successfully cast.
    • Determining Failure: 
      • Catastrophic Failure occurs if all Arcana checks fail. 
        • An explosion centering from a point at the center of all participating casters with a radius of (n*2) feet * 5 dealing (n*2)d12 force damage where n is the level of the spell that was attempted.
        • All participants suffer 3 levels of exhaustion
      • Major Failure occurs if half or more, but not all, Arcana checks fail.
        • An explosion centering from a point at the center of all participating casters with a radius of n feet * 5 dealing nd12 force damage where n is the level of the spell that was attempted.
        • All participants suffer 2 levels of exhaustion
      • Minor Failure occurs if at least 1, but less than half, of all Arcana checks fail.
        • All participants suffer 1 level of exhaustion

Spell Changes

Enlarge/Reduce

When enlarged you can double your Strength modifier on Strength checks, saves, and attacks.

Spell Experiments

Components

Recipes

Each recipe is compirsed of 3 parts:

  1. School: There are 8 schools from which to choose.
  2. Number: Pick any number wthin the range for the PC's specified class and level.
  3. Combination: State the order of the 3 components: Somatic, Verbal, and Material.

Rules

  1. Spells that are learned at first level have their Recipes given to the players.
  2. Spells that are given to classes through class/subclass/race features have their Recipes given to the players.
  3. A Magical Experiment takes 1 hour to perform and characters may do them as often as they like and can perform no other tasks when performing a Magical Experiment.
  4. Spell Scrolls that are found, assuming your class can cast it, teach you the Recipe of said spell. So, you gain a single-use scroll and a Spell Recipe.
  5. Generate at least 1 set of tables per class in your party. E.G. if you have 2 wizards in your party, they can share tables if you want, but a sorcerer and a warlock cannot share a table as they have access to different spells.

Step-by-step

  1. The player chooses a school from which they are able to cast spells, I.E. Conjuration, Necromancy, Evocation, Abjuration, Transmutation, Divination, Enchantment, or Illusion
  2. The GM then opens said school's sheet. (Generated here)
  3. The player chooses a number within the range for said school. Be sure to limit this range to spells the player can actually cast.
  4. The GM scrolls to that number.
  5. The player then plays the cards in a certain permutation, I.E. SVM, VMS, MSV, MVS, VSM, SMV.
  6. The GM looks up that permutation and describes the outcome to the player.
